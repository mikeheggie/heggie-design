# Designer — Clarifying Questions

Open questions from the brief that we need answers to before design moves forward. Grouped by what's blocking and what's shaping.

---

## 1. Positioning & voice

1. **One-line pitch.** The brief says "senior design engineer for agencies" — what's the *sharper* version that goes above the fold? (e.g. "I ship Webflow + Next.js sites that don't need a handoff" vs. "Design engineer for agencies that want fewer meetings".)
2. **Tone.** Confident-and-dry, warm-and-personal, or punchy-and-loud? Pick one — it sets type, color, and copy density.
3. **Differentiator.** What's the *one* thing an agency founder should remember 10 minutes after closing the tab? Is it the repo-as-demo concept, the breadth (design→code→ship), the speed, or the taste?
4. **Self-reference.** First person ("I build…"), third person ("Mike builds…"), or brand-style ("heggie.design builds…")?

## 2. Audience precision

5. **Agency archetype.** Webflow-heavy boutique, Next.js product studio, or Sanity/headless-CMS shop? They hire differently and value different proof.
6. **Decision-maker.** Founder/principal, design director, or eng lead? Changes whether we lead with craft, systems thinking, or shipping velocity.
7. **Geography / remote.** US-only, EU, global remote? Affects timezone signaling and whether we surface availability.

## 3. Home page structure

8. **Sections needed.** Confirm the stack: hero → proof/work → services or capabilities → about → testimonials → CTA. Anything we're cutting or adding (e.g. writing, process, tools)?
9. **Work to feature.** Which 3–6 projects? Are these real client engagements, side projects, or recreated/mock case studies? Do we have permission + assets for each?
10. **Case study depth.** Thumbnails-only, one-pager per project, or full case studies? Big design + scope implication.
11. **Proof artifacts.** Logos, testimonials, metrics, GitHub activity, dribbble-style shots — which exist, which are we mocking, which are off-limits?

## 4. The "repo is the demo" idea

12. **How visible on the live site?** Subtle footer link to the repo, prominent "view source" CTA, or a dedicated `/process` or `/build-log` page that renders git history?
13. **Does the visitor need to clone to get it,** or do we surface the workspace structure (designer / developer / marketer / pm) on the site itself?
14. **Is this a *primary* selling point** (front-and-center) or a *delight* layer for the small % who dig in? Affects how loud the design treatment is.

## 5. Book-a-call CTA

15. **Booking tool.** Cal.com, Calendly, custom form → email? Affects embed design and trust styling.
16. **Qualifying questions** on the booking form, or frictionless 1-click? Tradeoff between lead quality and conversion.
17. **CTA placement.** Sticky header, hero-only, after every section, or floating? Brief says "every page drives to it" — how aggressive?
18. **Secondary CTA?** "Email me" / "See the repo" / "Read the build log" as a softer option for not-ready-to-book visitors?

## 6. Visual direction

19. **Reference sites.** 3–5 sites Mike loves (and 2 he hates) — the fastest way to align on aesthetic.
20. **Type system.** Editorial/serif-driven, monospaced/dev-coded, sans-systems, or display-led? Pick a vibe.
21. **Color.** Mono + one accent, full palette, dark-only, light-only, or theme-toggle?
22. **Motion.** Restrained (fades, hovers), expressive (scroll-driven, GSAP-level), or none?
23. **Imagery.** Photo-of-Mike, no photo, abstract graphics, screenshots-of-work, or all of the above?
24. **Density.** Spacious editorial, dense info-rich, or somewhere between? Affects how much copy fits per screen.

## 7. Blog (3 foundational posts)

25. **Topics confirmed?** What are the 3 posts? Need titles to design article templates and OG cards.
26. **Article template needs.** Code blocks, embedded demos, image galleries, callouts, footnotes, table of contents — which?
27. **Author treatment.** Bylined with photo + bio, or anonymous-ish under the brand?

## 8. Paid channel mock

28. **Ad formats.** Static social, video, search ads, LinkedIn sponsored — which surfaces are we designing creatives for?
29. **Landing page variants.** How many, and what are they testing (headline, offer, social proof)?
30. **Lead capture.** Same booking form as the home page, or a lighter "get in touch" form first?
31. **Nurture sequence.** Email-only, or include retargeting ad designs too? How many emails?

## 9. SEO channel mock

32. **Are the 3 blog posts the SEO posts,** or separate?
33. **Target queries.** What informational searches are we ranking for? Affects metadata, schema, internal linking design.
34. **Programmatic / template-driven posts** in scope, or hand-crafted only?

## 10. System & components

35. **Component scope for T+24h.** Bare minimum (buttons, inputs, cards, nav, footer) or a fuller system (data display, forms, modals, empty states)?
36. **Design tokens / theming.** Light + dark, or just one? Tokens documented in Figma, in code, or both?
37. **Accessibility bar.** WCAG AA as baseline — confirm? Anything stricter (focus styles, reduced-motion, keyboard nav demos)?

## 11. Brand assets

38. **Logo / wordmark.** Exists, needs designing, or wordmark-only? Favicon + OG image set needed.
39. **Domain treatment.** "heggie.design" as the wordmark, or a separate brand name?

## 12. Constraints

40. **Time budget for design.** T+24h for launch — how many of those hours are design vs. build? What's the cut-line if we run over?
41. **Off-limits.** Anything we explicitly should *not* design (e.g. "no AI-generated illustrations", "no stock photos", "no carousels")?
42. **Existing assets to reuse.** Any prior portfolio, Figma libraries, or brand work we should pull from rather than starting fresh?

---

## What I'd recommend tackling first

Answers to **1, 2, 5, 8, 9, 12, 14, 19** unblock the home-page wireframe.
Answers to **3, 7, 11, 25, 38** unblock the visual system.
Everything else can be parallelized once those are settled.
